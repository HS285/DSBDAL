import pandas as pd
import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
df=pd.read_csv("titanic.csv")  
df
df.head()
df.tail()
df.info()
df['Fare'].describe()
plt.figure()
sns.histplot(df['Fare'],bins=30,kde=True)
plt.title('Fare Distribution')
plt.xlabel('Fare')
plt.ylabel('Frequency')
plt.show()

plt.figure()
sns.boxplot(x='Survived',y='Fare',data=df)
plt.title('Fare vs Survived ')
plt.show()

plt.figure()
sns.boxplot(x='Pclass',y='Fare',data=df)
plt.title('Pclass Vs Fare')
plt.show()

print(df['Fare'].skew())

🔹 4. Analyze Distribution
Most passengers paid low fares (0–50 range)
Very few passengers paid high fares (above 100)
The data is not evenly distributed

5. Identify Skewness

👉 The Fare distribution is positively skewed (right-skewed)

Meaning:

Tail is longer on the right side
Few passengers paid very high fares
