import pandas as pd
import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load the Titanic dataset
df=pd.read_csv("titanic.csv")  # Ensure the dataset is in the same directory
# Display basic information and first few rows to inspect the dataset
display(df.info())
display(df.head())
#Plot boxplot for numeric feature
plt.figure(figsize=(7,5))
sns.boxplot(y='age', data=df)

plt.title("Boxplot of Age")
plt.show()

# Plot a box plot for Age distribution by Gender and Survival Status (Compare)
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
sns.boxplot(x='Sex', y='Age', hue='Survived', data=df, palette='pastel')
plt.xlabel('Gender')
plt.ylabel('Age')
plt.title('Age Distribution by Gender and Survival Status')
plt.show()

4. Identify Outliers

👉 In the boxplot:

Points outside whiskers = outliers
In age, outliers may include:
Very young infants
Very old passengers
🔹 5. Observations
Median age of passengers is around 28–30 years
Females show higher survival rate
Younger passengers had slightly better survival chances
Age distribution is spread with some extreme values (outliers)
🔹 6. Inferences
Gender played a major role in survival (females prioritized)
Age has moderate impact (children slightly favored)
Presence of outliers indicates diverse age group onboard
Boxplot helps in visualizing spread and detecting anomalies
