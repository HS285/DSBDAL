import pandas as pd
import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
df=sns.load_dataset('titanic')
#df=pd.read_csv("titanic.csv")  
df
df.head()
df.tail()
df.describe()
df.info()

sns.boxplot(y=df['age'])
plt.title('Boxplot of age')
plt.show()

sns.boxplot(y=df['fare'])
plt.title('Boxplot of Fare')
plt.show()

sns.boxplot(x='sex',y='age',hue='survived',data=df)
plt.title('Age vs Gender vs Survival')
plt.xlabel("Gender")
plt.ylabel("Age")
plt.show()

sns.boxplot(y=df['fare'])
plt.show()

4. Identify Outliers
Boxplot automatically shows outliers as points outside whiskers.

🔹 5. Observations
1. Boxplot of Age

The age boxplot shows the distribution of passenger ages. Most passengers are between young adult and middle age. Some very young and older passengers may appear as outliers.

2. Boxplot of Fare

The fare boxplot shows that most passengers paid lower fares, but some passengers paid very high fares. These high fare values are outliers.

3. Age vs Gender vs Survival

This boxplot compares age with gender and survival status.

🔹 6. Inferences
Both male and female passengers are compared based on age.
Survival patterns differ between males and females.
Female passengers generally had better survival chances than male passengers.
Age distribution varies slightly among survived and non-survived passengers.
